Eliminate an implausible outcome from the prediction space, with explicit reasoning citing which phase findings rule it out.
Inputs: `outcome_description` (required — what cannot happen), `elimination_reasoning` (required — why, citing specific phase findings), `citing_phases` (array of {phase, finding} objects).
Elimination reasoning must cite real phase findings already recorded in the analysis; do not assert eliminations based on intuition alone.
Eliminated outcomes are often the most valuable analytical output — "Bilateral settlement is eliminated because Phase 4 shows trust infrastructure is destroyed" constrains the prediction space more usefully than any positive forecast.
